just how much is a B I L L I O N ?


                        Wealth inequality in the US has reached critical levels. Around 42 million Americans fell below the poverty line in 2018, according to the US census bureau. Meanwhile, the world’s billionaires sit on massive
                        stores of wealth, hoarding resources that could be used to all our benefit. Their wealth has the power to change lives, as well as our planet itself– UN scientists estimate that $300 billion could halt climate change
                        for 20 years. Jeff Bezos, Bill Gates, Warren Buffett, Larry Ellison, and Mark Zuckerberg could choose today to pause climate change until 2040, and even after doing so, they would each have $29 billion left over.
                        Instead, they buy luxury cars and homes, benefit from huge tax breaks, and sit on stores of wealth too massive to ever reasonably spend.

                        The American myth of meritocracy forces us to believe that billionaires have earned their lifestyle through hard work and ingenuity. In reality, it is far more likely that most of these men were simply in the right
                        place at the right time. In a nation that claims to provide equal opportunity for all, we must ask ourselves why we are willing to let families go hungry, live paycheck to paycheck, and sleep on the street in the
                        face of such excess.
